About Propane Services in Fabens, TX

Fabens is a rural agricultural community about 30 miles southeast of El Paso in the lower Rio Grande valley, where pecan orchards and irrigated farmland surround the small town center. Propane here isn't just for grilling — it powers irrigation engines, agricultural dryers, and portable heaters used on farms throughout the valley. Local feed stores and fuel suppliers often stock propane alongside other agricultural supplies, making it possible to handle multiple errands in one stop.

That relationship-oriented supplier base also means it's worth calling ahead, especially for larger agricultural cylinder fills — local dealers often accommodate regular customers with flexible scheduling even outside posted hours.

Frequently Asked Questions – Propane in Fabens

Is it better to refill or exchange my propane tank in Fabens?

In Fabens, refilling is strongly preferred over exchange. Exchange kiosks are scarce in this rural area — most residents depend on local refill stations at feed stores or fuel stops. Refilling gives you a full 20-lb fill and costs less per gallon than an equivalent exchange. For agricultural users with 100-lb or larger cylinders, refill is the only practical option since exchange programs only handle standard 20-lb grill tanks.

How much does propane cost in Fabens, TX?

Retail propane refill prices in Fabens typically range from $3.00 to $4.50 per gallon for standard grill tanks. Agricultural bulk fills may be negotiated at lower per-gallon rates, especially for regular farm accounts. Because Fabens has fewer suppliers than central El Paso, there's less price competition — it's worth confirming the current rate by phone before making the drive. Prices tend to rise in fall and winter when residential heating demand increases across the lower valley.

Where can I get propane in Fabens if I run out on a weekend?

Propane availability in Fabens is more limited than in larger cities, and some local dealers keep shorter weekend hours. Your best option is to check the listings on this page and call ahead before driving over. Feed stores and fuel stops that carry propane in Fabens often have the most consistent hours. If no local option is available, the nearest reliable fallback is Socorro or the southeastern El Paso area, about 15–20 miles up State Highway 20.

Can farmers and agricultural operations get propane delivered in Fabens?

Yes. Propane delivery to farms and agricultural operations in Fabens and the lower El Paso Valley is available through several regional suppliers. Deliveries commonly cover 100-lb cylinders and larger stationary tanks used for irrigation engines, pecan crop dryers, and greenhouse heating. Bulk delivery pricing for agricultural accounts is typically negotiated separately from retail rates — contact suppliers directly to ask about farm delivery routes and scheduling in the Fabens area.

How far is it to the nearest propane station from Fabens?

Fabens itself has a small number of local propane sources. If those are unavailable, Socorro is approximately 12–15 miles northwest on State Highway 20, and the southeastern El Paso supply corridor (Montana Avenue area) is roughly 25–30 miles away. For residents of the lower valley who make regular trips into El Paso, it's worth planning propane refills as part of that trip rather than relying solely on Fabens-area options during peak demand periods.
