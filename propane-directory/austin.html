About Propane Services in Austin

Austin's rapid growth has expanded propane demand across both the city core and surrounding suburbs like Round Rock, Cedar Park, Pflugerville, and Buda. Many Austin residents use propane for outdoor grilling, patio heaters, and gas cooktops, while rural properties in the nearby Hill Country rely heavily on propane for home heating and water heating. The city's active outdoor and barbecue culture makes it one of the highest per-capita propane markets in Central Texas.

With Austin's tech-savvy population, several local propane suppliers now offer online scheduling for tank deliveries and large refills. For standard grill tank needs, exchange kiosks are widely available at grocery and hardware stores throughout the metro area.

Frequently Asked Questions – Propane in Austin

What is the difference between propane refill and exchange in Austin?

A propane refill fills your existing tank to its rated capacity — you pay for the actual gas added. A propane exchange swaps your empty tank for a pre-filled one, but exchange cylinders are usually only filled to about 15 lbs, not the full 20-lb capacity. For regular grill users, refill is almost always the better deal per gallon.

How much does propane cost in Austin?

Austin propane refill prices typically range from $3.00 to $4.50 per gallon at retail locations. Prices fluctuate with energy markets and seasonal demand. During summer months, prices are generally lower; winter heating season can push prices higher. Calling ahead to confirm the current rate is always recommended.

Can I get propane delivered to my home in Austin?

Yes. Several Austin-area suppliers offer residential delivery for large tanks, particularly for customers in Cedar Park, Round Rock, and rural areas outside the city. Look for suppliers listing "refill" services and call to ask about delivery options and minimum order requirements.

Where can I exchange a propane tank in Austin?

Propane exchange tanks are available at most major hardware stores, home improvement retailers, and many grocery stores across Austin. Listings marked "Exchange" on this page carry pre-filled tanks you can swap on the spot — no appointment needed. Just bring your empty cylinder and pick up a filled replacement.
