April 28, 2026

Propane prices vary more than most people realize — by region, by season, by whether you're refilling a small grill tank or a whole-home tank, and by whether you're buying at a retail kiosk or through a local dealer. Why Propane Prices Fluctuate

Propane is a byproduct of natural gas processing and oil refining, so its price tracks crude oil and natural gas markets. Several factors push prices up or down:

  • Crude oil prices — the biggest driver of long-term propane pricing
  • Season — prices typically rise in fall and winter as heating demand increases, and drop in spring and summer
  • Distance from supply — rural areas pay more due to transportation costs
  • Local competition — areas with more dealers tend to have lower prices
  • Weather events — a hard freeze or hurricane can spike propane prices regionally within days
  • Inventory levels — national propane storage figures (published weekly by the EIA) affect wholesale prices

Refill vs Exchange: What's the Better Deal?

For a 20lb grill tank, refilling at a local propane dealer almost always beats the exchange kiosk on price. Here's why:

  • A refill fills your tank to the full 20 lbs of propane
  • Exchange tanks from Blue Rhino and AmeriGas are typically filled to only 15 lbs (75% capacity) as a cost-saving measure, despite being sold as "20lb" tanks
  • At $25 for an exchange vs $20 for a refill, you're paying more for less propane

The exchange wins on convenience — you can grab one at Walmart or Home Depot at any hour without special equipment. But if you have an extra 10 minutes and a local refill station nearby, the refill is the better deal. See our full breakdown: Propane Exchange vs Refill.

How to Get the Best Propane Price

  • Buy in the off-season — propane is cheapest in late spring and summer when heating demand is low. If you have a large tank, fill it in May or June
  • Compare local dealers — prices between dealers in the same city can vary by $0.50–$1.00/gallon
  • Ask about budget billing — many residential propane suppliers offer budget plans that average your annual usage into equal monthly payments, protecting you from winter price spikes
  • Pre-buy programs — some dealers let you lock in a price for the season in advance
  • Avoid running out — emergency fills when your tank hits empty often come with premium pricing and delivery fees

How Much Propane Does a 20lb Tank Hold?

A standard 20lb propane tank holds approximately 4.7 gallons of liquid propane when full. At $3.00/gallon for a refill, that's about $14 worth of propane — which explains why refills from a dealer typically run $15–$22, with the markup covering the service and overhead.
