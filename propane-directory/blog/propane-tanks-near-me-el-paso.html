For most El Paso residents, the 20-lb cylinder is the everyday workhorse. It's what fits standard gas grills and most portable heaters, and it's the size accepted by exchange programs. Anything larger requires a dedicated refill station or bulk delivery service.

Where to Buy a Propane Tank in El Paso

If you don't already own a cylinder, you have a few options for getting your first tank:

  • Exchange program purchase: Blue Rhino and AmeriGas sell pre-filled 20-lb cylinders outright at Walmart, Home Depot, Lowe's, and select HEB locations across El Paso. Expect to pay $45–$60 for the tank plus propane. It's the most accessible option, though not the cheapest per gallon.
  • Hardware stores: Ace Hardware and similar stores sell empty cylinders for $30–$40. You then take the empty tank to a refill station. Buying empty and filling locally typically gives you a better per-gallon rate going forward.
  • Propane dealers: Local propane dealers sometimes sell cylinders directly, often paired with your first fill at a discounted rate.
  • Online / big-box retail: Home Depot and Walmart carry empty cylinders in store and online, ranging from 20-lb cylinders to 40-lb and larger.

Refill Stations vs. Exchange Cages in El Paso

El Paso has good coverage of both refill stations and exchange cages. The practical choice depends on your situation:

  • Use a refill station if your tank is in good condition, you use propane regularly, and a station is accessible within your normal routine. You'll pay $8–$15 less per fill and get more gas.
  • Use an exchange cage if you run out unexpectedly, your tank is old or expired, it's late at night, or you're buying your first tank. Exchange cages at Walmart and gas stations are available across El Paso seven days a week.

Propane for Specific Uses in El Paso

Patio and space heaters: El Paso winters are mild by most standards, but temperatures in the 20s°F on December and January nights make outdoor propane heaters genuinely useful. A single 20-lb tank runs a standard patio heater for 10–14 hours on medium setting.

Ask about contract terms — some dealers lock in prices seasonally, which can save money if rates spike.

How to Check If Your Tank Is Expired

Propane cylinders have a stamped manufacture date on the collar (the metal ring around the valve). Cylinders are required to be recertified 12 years from that date. An expired cylinder may be refused at refill stations — but exchange programs will swap it out for a newer one. Read the collar stamp: it'll show a month and year in the format MM-YY. If the year is 2013 or earlier, your tank is due for recertification or replacement. See our tank recertification guide for details on what the options are.
