What Propane Refill Costs in El Paso

Propane refill prices in El Paso typically run $3.00–$4.50 per gallon depending on the provider, your tank size, and market conditions. A standard 20-lb grill cylinder holds about 4.7 gallons, so expect to pay $14–$21 for a full refill. Larger tanks — 40 lb, 100 lb — are priced by the gallon and get proportionally more cost-effective the larger you go.

Prices in El Paso tend to track closely with West Texas regional wholesale rates. The city sits at a distribution crossroads due to its location on the I-10 corridor and proximity to the New Mexico border, which generally keeps supply stable and prices competitive compared to more rural parts of the state.

What to Expect When You Get a Propane Refill

If you've never used a refill station before, the process is simple:

  1. Drive up with your empty or partially-empty tank in your vehicle (upright, with the valve closed).
  2. An attendant — or sometimes a self-serve pump — connects the fill hose to your tank's OPD valve.
  3. The tank is filled by weight or by the gallon, stopping automatically when it reaches 80% capacity (the safe fill level for liquid propane).
  4. You pay for exactly the gallons added. If you had gas left in the tank, you only pay for what went in.

The whole process takes 5–10 minutes. Most stations accept card and cash. Some dealers will have a minimum charge or charge a small connection fee — call ahead if you're unsure.

El Paso's Climate and Propane Demand

El Paso's high desert climate means propane gets used year-round. Summer outdoor cooking season runs long — March through October at minimum — while winter nights can dip into the 20s°F, making patio heaters, space heaters, and fireplace inserts a genuine necessity from November through February. Demand spikes in late fall as residents fire up heating equipment for the first time, so prices can tick up slightly in October and November. If you fill your tanks in August or September, you'll typically get the best rates before the seasonal bump.

Tips for Getting the Best Propane Price in El Paso

  • Call ahead on price: Unlike exchange cages, refill station pricing isn't always posted online. A quick call can confirm the current per-gallon rate before you drive.
  • Fill before you're empty: Partial fills cost the same per gallon as a full fill, but keeping a tank from completely emptying extends its lifespan and avoids a purge fee some stations charge on bone-dry tanks.
  • Buy in bulk when prices are low: If you have a larger tank (100 gal+), filling it during summer when demand is lower can lock in better rates.
  • Check for independent dealers: Locally-owned propane dealers in El Paso often price slightly below national franchise locations. Our directory specifically lists independent stations alongside chains.
