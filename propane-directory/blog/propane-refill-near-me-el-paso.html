Propane Refill Near Me in El Paso, TX: Where to Go & What to Pay

April 24, 2026

If you're searching for a propane refill near El Paso, TX, you have more options than most people realize. El Paso is well-served by local propane dealers, hardware stores, and gas suppliers — and refilling your cylinder locally will almost always cost less than swapping at an exchange cage. This guide covers where to go, what you'll pay, and what to bring.

Where to Get a Propane Refill in El Paso

El Paso has propane refill locations spread across the city, from the Lower Valley to the Westside and the Northeast. Here's where to look:

  • Dedicated propane dealers: Local suppliers like Ferrellgas, AmeriGas bulk dealers, and independent propane companies fill tanks of all sizes — from standard 20-lb grill cylinders up to 100-lb and larger. These offer the lowest per-gallon prices.
  • U-Haul locations: Several El Paso U-Haul centers offer propane refill service. They fill by weight and are a reliable option for 20-lb and 33-lb cylinders.
  • Hardware and home improvement stores: Select Tractor Supply locations in the El Paso area offer propane refill service at the outdoor fill station.
  • RV dealers and campgrounds: If you're near Fort Bliss or passing through on I-10, some RV-oriented businesses along the highway offer refill for larger tanks.

Refill vs Exchange: The Cost Difference in El Paso

The exchange cages outside Walmart, Home Depot, and most El Paso grocery stores offer convenience, but the cost difference is significant:

OptionGas You ReceiveTypical Cost (El Paso)Cost Per Gallon
Refill (local dealer)~20 lbs / 4.7 gal full fill$16–$21$3.40–$4.50
Exchange (Blue Rhino / AmeriGas)~15 lbs / 3.5 gal only$24–$28$4.80–$5.50

Exchange programs fill tanks to only 15 lbs — not the full 20-lb capacity. That means you're paying more per gallon and getting less gas. On a single tank, you could save $7–$12 by refilling instead of exchanging. Over a full grilling or camping season, that adds up fast.

What to Bring to a Propane Refill Station

  • Your empty or partially-filled propane cylinder (any size)
  • Check the collar stamp: tanks more than 12 years old may need recertification before a dealer will fill them. Look for the date stamped on the collar (e.g., "06 14" means June 2014 — recertification was due June 2026).
  • Payment — most El Paso dealers accept cash and card
  • Transport the tank upright in your vehicle, ideally in a truck bed or with windows cracked
