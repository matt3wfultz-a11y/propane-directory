Horizon City Propane Locations

About Propane Services in Horizon City

Horizon City is one of the fastest-growing communities in the El Paso metro area, stretching along Interstate 10 east of the city limits. The rapid residential development here has brought new commercial corridors with propane exchange kiosks at grocery stores and hardware retailers, making quick tank swaps convenient for the growing population. At the same time, the older neighborhoods and larger lots common in parts of Horizon City mean many residents use 100-lb or larger stationary tanks for home heating supplements and outdoor kitchens.

The desert climate — hot summers and cool winters — drives seasonal propane demand for patio heaters in fall and spring and for grilling year-round. Because Horizon City sits directly on I-10, RV travelers passing through also use local propane stops before heading deeper into West Texas. Refill stations in the area serve both the residential base and a steady stream of travelers needing motorhome and travel trailer fills.

Frequently Asked Questions – Propane in Horizon City

Is propane refill or exchange better in Horizon City?

Horizon City has a good mix of both options. Exchange kiosks have expanded alongside the community's commercial growth, so you'll find Blue Rhino and AmeriGas cages at several grocery and hardware stores. But refill is still the better value — exchange tanks are typically pre-filled to only 15 lbs while a refill tops your tank to its full 20-lb capacity. If you grill regularly, the savings from refilling add up quickly. RV owners with large coach tanks should always refill rather than exchange, since exchange programs don't accept oversized cylinders.

How much does propane cost in Horizon City?

Retail refill prices in Horizon City generally fall in the $3.00–$4.50 per gallon range, consistent with the broader El Paso metro area. Because Horizon City has been growing, competition among local suppliers is increasing — which can mean slightly more variation in pricing than in older, more established communities. It's worth a quick call to compare rates between the two or three nearest options. Prices tend to edge up in late October and November when residents fire up patio heaters as desert nights cool down.

Where can I exchange a propane tank in Horizon City?

Exchange cages (Blue Rhino, AmeriGas) are available at grocery stores and hardware retailers along the main commercial corridors near I-10 and Horizon Boulevard. The listings on this page marked "Exchange" show current locations. No appointment is needed — just bring your empty or low tank and swap it for a pre-filled one at the cage. If you're just passing through on I-10 and need a quick swap, these commercial corridor locations are the fastest option.

Do propane suppliers in Horizon City fill RV tanks?

Yes. Several suppliers in Horizon City and just west along I-10 can fill RV and motorhome propane tanks, including ASME-stamped coach tanks that exchange programs won't accept. If you're traveling I-10 and need an RV fill before heading into the open stretches of West Texas, Horizon City is a practical stop. Call ahead to confirm the supplier has an accessible fill station for large vehicles — some locations require pull-through access for Class A motorhomes.

Can I get propane delivered to my home in Horizon City?

Yes. Several El Paso-area propane suppliers offer residential delivery to Horizon City for 100-lb tanks and larger stationary units. Delivery is especially useful for homeowners with permanent tanks for outdoor kitchens, whole-home generators, or pool heaters. Contact suppliers directly to ask about delivery scheduling, minimum fill amounts, and whether they service your specific neighborhood — Horizon City's outer edges can be farther from some supplier routes.
