About Propane Services in Deming, NM

Deming is a gateway community along I-10 in southwestern New Mexico, serving local residents, farmers, and a large RV traveler community. The area's cold winters and remote location make propane an essential fuel for heating, cooking, and backup power. Several local suppliers in Deming have been serving the community for decades, offering both retail refills and bulk delivery.

Deming is a popular overnight stop for RV travelers crossing between Texas and Arizona, and propane suppliers here are accustomed to filling motorhome tanks and travel trailers. Call ahead for large fills to confirm availability.

Frequently Asked Questions – Propane in Deming

Is Deming a good propane stop for RV travelers on I-10?

Yes — Deming is one of the most important RV propane stops on the I-10 corridor between El Paso and Tucson. The stretch from El Paso to Tucson covers roughly 320 miles of high desert with very limited services, and Deming sits almost exactly at the midpoint. Local suppliers are experienced with motorhome and travel trailer fills, and several can accommodate large coach tanks without advance scheduling. If you're heading west, topping off in Deming is strongly advisable — Lordsburg (60 miles west) has minimal services, and the next reliable propane hub after that is Tucson, another 160 miles away.

How much does propane cost in Deming, NM?

Retail propane prices in Deming typically run between $3.25 and $5.00 per gallon. Prices here tend to be slightly higher than in Las Cruces due to Deming's more remote position in the distribution network — the city relies on supply routes from both El Paso and Tucson. Prices spike in winter months when heating demand rises across Luna County. Because Deming has a small number of suppliers, there's limited price competition, so calling ahead to confirm the current rate is especially worthwhile before making the trip.

Can I fill my motorhome or RV propane tank in Deming?

Yes. Deming's propane suppliers regularly serve RV travelers passing through on I-10, including owners of Class A motorhomes with large ASME-stamped coach tanks that exchange programs cannot accept. The town's long history as an I-10 travel stop means local dealers are accustomed to fast turnaround on motorhome fills. If you have a very large coach tank or multiple cylinders to fill, calling ahead is a good idea so the supplier can confirm they have sufficient bulk supply and an accessible fill point for your rig.

What are the hours for propane suppliers in Deming?

Deming's propane options are fewer than in larger cities, and supplier hours vary. Some local dealers operate primarily on weekday business hours while others have weekend availability for travelers. Because the town has a small pool of propane sources, running out on a Sunday evening with no open supplier is a real risk. Check the contact information in the listings above and call ahead — especially if you plan to arrive on a weekend or holiday. Confirming hours before you arrive prevents a wasted detour off I-10.

Do Deming propane suppliers deliver to rural Luna County?

Yes. Some suppliers in Deming offer bulk delivery to rural properties in Luna County, including farms, ranches, and remote residences outside the city limits where propane is often the only practical fuel source. Delivery scheduling typically requires advance notice, and minimum fill amounts apply. Contact suppliers directly to confirm they serve your area and to ask about delivery lead times — rural routes in Luna County may only be scheduled once or twice a week depending on the supplier's route calendar.
